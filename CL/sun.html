Your Prompt

Week 1 — Story Starter
"You open a door that wasn't there yesterday. Write the story of what happens next."
You can write a full short story, or just an exciting opening chapter. Your choice!
1

Plan Your Story

5 min

Fill in this plan on your paper before you start writing. Good writers plan first!

Who is the main character?Write their name + one thing about them
Where does the door appear?Bedroom? Garden? School?
What is behind the door?Another world? A secret room? The past?
What is the problem?Something goes wrong or is surprising
BeginningFirst thing that happens
MiddleThe exciting part
EndHow it finishes (happy, mysterious, cliffhanger?)
2

Write Your Story

20 min

Write on your lined paper. Aim for:

150–200 words minimum
Paragraphs — new idea, new paragraph
2+ "wow words" from the list below

Try to use some of these wow words in your story:

cascaded
trembling
peculiar
shimmered
hesitantly
ancient
sprawling
glimmered

Don't worry about making it perfect — write first, fix later. Keep going!

3

Self-Edit Checklist

5 min

Read your story back to yourself quietly. Tick each box when you're happy with it:

  • Capital letters — every sentence starts with one. Names too.
  • Full stops — every sentence ends with one (or ! or ?).
  • Speech marks — if anyone speaks, it's in "quotes".
  • Paragraphs — did you start a new paragraph for each new idea?
  • Varied sentences — are all your sentences the same length? Mix short and long.
  • Wow words — have you used at least 2 interesting or unusual words?
  • Show, don't tell — instead of "she was scared," can you show it? ("Her hands trembled.")
  • Does it make sense? — read it as if you've never seen it before.

Marking Bands

Top (15 pts bonus)Imaginative, structured, varied sentences, 2+ wow words used correctly, clear voice. Suton SET Stage 2 ready.
Good (9 pts bonus)Clear story with beginning/middle/end. At least one interesting word. Some varied punctuation.
Developing (3 pts bonus)Story present but underdeveloped. Mainly simple sentences. Spellings mostly correct.
Needs support (0 bonus)Incomplete or very short. Focus feedback on encouragement + one specific thing to try next time.

Key things to look for (Sutton SET Stage 2 criteria)

  • Varied sentence openers (not all starting with "I" or "The")
  • Show don't tell — does she describe feelings through action?
  • Paragraphing — new idea = new paragraph
  • Ambitious vocabulary — wow words used in correct context
  • Ending — is it satisfying? Surprising? Deliberate?

Feedback approach

  • Lead with a specific genuine positive ("I love the way you wrote…")
  • One clear improvement target only ("Next time, try…")
  • Link to Sutton SET — "This is exactly the kind of writing Wilson's is looking for"

Read This Passage

THE ROCK POOL

Maya pressed her nose close to the surface of the water and peered in. After an hour of clambering over barnacled rocks in the blazing August sun, she had finally found what she was looking for.

The pool was no bigger than a dinner table, but it contained an entire world. Strands of emerald seaweed swayed in the shallow current. Tiny silver fish darted between the stones, moving so fast they seemed to dissolve and reappear. A sea anemone — a splat of deep red — flexed its tentacles slowly, as though conducting an invisible orchestra.

Then Maya noticed something else. Tucked beneath an overhang of rock, perfectly camouflaged against the grey stone, sat a shore crab. Its shell was patterned with splashes of rust and cream, and its claws were raised in what seemed like a warning.

"Hello," Maya whispered.

The crab's eyes, perched on tiny stalks, swivelled towards her. For a moment they regarded each other — girl and crab — across the glittering surface. Then the crab retreated sideways into the shadows with surprising speed and disappeared.

Maya sat back on her heels, grinning. She pulled out her notebook and began to sketch everything she had seen. She would need to remember every detail. One day, she decided, she would be a marine biologist — a scientist who studies the sea. For now, though, a rock pool in Cornwall would do just fine.

Question 6 ⭐
⭐ STAR CHALLENGE
Author's craft
The writer describes the rock pool as containing "an entire world." Why do you think the writer chose this phrase? What effect does it have on the reader?
Think about what a "world" means — is it big or small? Why is that interesting? · Write 3–4 sentences · This is the hardest question!

Q1 — Retrieval (1 mark)

  • Shore crab / crab tucked under rock overhang / patterned shell with rust and cream / claws raised. Any two details = full mark.

Q2 — Retrieval (1 mark)

  • "blazing August sun" (accept "blazing sun"). Must use inverted commas for top mark.

Q3 — Inference (2 marks)

  • 1 mark: One valid inference with evidence (e.g. she was grinning / she clambered for an hour suggesting determination / she immediately sketched it).
  • 2 marks: Two distinct pieces of evidence with explanation.

Q4 — Inference (2 marks)

  • Crab seems defensive / guarded / wary — claws raised as a warning. Accept cautious, shy, protective. 1 mark for observation, 2 for explanation.

Q5 — Vocabulary (2 marks)

  • "Retreated" suggests the crab moved away from danger / like a soldier withdrawing from battle. Shows the crab felt threatened. "Moved" is neutral — "retreated" adds drama and implies the crab was in retreat, making it feel like a small battle.

Q6 ⭐ — Author's Craft (3 marks)

  • 1 mark: Identifies the contrast (pool is tiny but described as a "world").
  • 2 marks: Explains the effect — makes the reader appreciate how much life exists in a small space.
  • 3 marks: Connects to Maya's sense of wonder / the writer wants the reader to feel the same amazement Maya feels — that big things can hide in small places.

Total: 11 marks · Award points:

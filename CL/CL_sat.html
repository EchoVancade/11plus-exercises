The Butterfly of Willowmere

Maria had been exploring Willowmere Wood since before breakfast, her notebook clutched tightly under her arm. It was her twelfth birthday, but she had wanted to spend the afternoon exactly like this — alone among the oaks and birches, watching and listening.


She almost missed it. A flicker of deep blue between the ferns, low to the ground. Maria dropped to her knees. There, resting on a speckled leaf, was a butterfly unlike any she had ever seen. Its wings shimmered like oil on water — iridescent blues and purples shifting as she moved.


Heart hammering, she photographed it carefully with her phone, then cycled straight to the university. Professor Haines was at her desk when Maria knocked.


"Professor," Maria said, holding out her phone. "I found this in Willowmere."


The professor went very still. She leaned forward slowly, studying the image.


"This cannot be right," she whispered. "This species was declared extinct forty years ago."

📖 The Letter in the Library

Maya had been pulling books from the highest shelf when the envelope slipped out. It was old — the kind of old that made you careful how you held it — and sealed with a red wax stamp that had cracked in two. Her name was written on the front in a handwriting she did not recognise.

She sat down cross-legged on the dusty floor, her heart thumping in a way that made it difficult to think. Libraries were supposed to be quiet. Safe. This did not feel quiet or safe.

Inside was a single sheet of paper, and on it three words: Find the door.

Maya looked up. The library stretched around her in every direction, row upon row of shelves disappearing into the gloom. She had come here every weekend for three years. She knew exactly where the biography section was, where the broken radiator leaked, and where old Mr Briggs always fell asleep in his chair.

She had never noticed a door.

Outside, rain hammered against the tall windows, running down the glass in grey rivers. The lights flickered once. Mr Briggs stirred in his chair, muttered something, then settled back into sleep.

Maya folded the note carefully and put it in her pocket. Her hands, she noticed, were not shaking. She was, she realised with some surprise, completely calm.

She stood up. She was going to find that door.

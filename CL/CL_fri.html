⚡ TODAY'S WEAPONS▼
E5.2 Inference: The writer never tells you the answer directly — you have to be a detective. Clues are hidden in what characters do, avoid, or hesitate over. When you find a clue, ask: what does this reveal about how someone feels or thinks?
M2.1 Percentages: Weapon: convert the percent to a decimal first. 35 percent = 0.35. Then multiply: 0.35 times 80 = 28. For "percent of" questions, decimal times the number always works.
E5.4 Mood and Tone: Mood = how the reader feels. Tone = the writer's attitude. Look at the adjectives and verbs chosen — are they dark and heavy (tension), light and bright (joy), or flat and dull (sadness)?

✍️ Mission Pack M-C — The Character Reveal
Mission Pack M-C
The Character Reveal
Today you are revealing who a character is through what they do — not what you tell the reader.
1
Show Don't Tell (W1.1): Instead of writing "she was scared", show it through actions — "her hands shook as she reached for the handle." Readers should feel the emotion, not just be told it.
2
Character Voice (W4.4): Give your character a distinctive way of speaking or thinking. Their words should feel unique to them — not like anyone else in the story.
3
Physical Detail (W2.3): Pick one precise physical detail that tells us something about the character — a worn badge, bitten nails, a pencil tucked behind the ear. Specific beats vague every time.

Write 3 to 5 sentences. A new student arrives at school. Use all three techniques to reveal their character — without ever telling us directly what kind of person they are.

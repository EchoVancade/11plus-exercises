Your Story Today

🐉🔥
A tiny dragon has lost its flame. Help it find it!
You get to decide where the flame went, how the dragon feels, and how the story ends. It's your story!
1

Draw Your Dragon First!

5 min

Before you write, draw your dragon on your paper. This helps you imagine the story!

🎨
Draw your dragon on your paper
What colour is it? Is it tiny or huge? Does it look sad without its flame?

Give your dragon a name! You'll use it in your sentences.

2

Write Your Story

20 min

Use these sentence starters to help you. Finish each one on your paper — then keep writing if you want to!

Sentence 1 — Who
My dragon is called __________ and it lives __________
Finish the sentence. Tell us about your dragon!
Sentence 2 — The problem
One day, __________ woke up and its flame had gone because __________
Where did the flame go? Make up a reason!
Sentence 3 — The search
The dragon looked everywhere. It searched __________ and __________
Name two places the dragon looked!
Sentence 4 — The happy ending
At last, __________ found its flame! It felt __________
How did it end? How did the dragon feel?
⭐ Bonus sentence — try this too!
Write one more sentence of your own. Tell us something surprising or exciting!
No starter this time — it's all yours!
3

Add Describing Words!

5 min

Read your sentences back. Can you add a describing word to make them more interesting?

🌟 Describing words you could use:

Try adding one of these to your sentences — or make up your own!

tiny

You earn a ⭐ for every sentence you finish — and an extra ⭐ for each describing word you add!

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

What to look for (age 7 appropriate)

  • 4 sentences completed = full marks for structure
  • Bonus sentence attempted = +1 mark
  • Each describing word used correctly = +0.5 mark (up to +2)
  • Dragon named and given personality = great sign of creative engagement
  • Capital letters at sentence starts = basic standard for Y2/3
  • Full stops present = 1 mark

Feedback approach for IL

  • Name a specific word she used well: "I love the word 'sparkly' — perfect for a dragon!"
  • One gentle nudge only: "Next time, can you make the ending even more exciting?"
  • Celebrate the drawing too — it's part of the creative process

Points: 8 base + up to 4 bonus for quality + drawing

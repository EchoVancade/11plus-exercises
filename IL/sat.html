The Lion and the Mouse
Aesop's Fables · Adapted

One afternoon, a tiny mouse was running across the grass when — whoops! — he ran right over a sleeping lion's nose.

The lion woke up with a roar. He slapped his giant paw down and caught the little mouse.

"Please let me go!" squeaked the mouse. "I'm so sorry! If you spare me, I promise I will help you one day."

The lion laughed. What could a tiny mouse ever do to help a mighty lion? But he felt kind, so he lifted his paw and let the mouse go free.

Some days later, the lion was walking through the forest when — SNAP! — he fell into a hunter's net. He roared and struggled, but he could not get free.

The little mouse heard the roaring. He ran to help. With his sharp little teeth, he nibbled and nibbled at the ropes until — at last — the net fell apart.

"You laughed when I said I would help you," said the mouse. "But even a small friend can do great things!"

The lion smiled warmly. "You are right, little friend. I will never laugh at a small kindness again."

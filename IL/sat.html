Today's Story

The Lion and the Mouse
Aesop's Fables

One afternoon, a tiny mouse was running across the grass when — whoops! — he ran right over a sleeping lion's nose.

The lion woke up with a roar. He slapped his giant paw down and caught the little mouse.

"Please let me go!" squeaked the mouse. "I'm so sorry! If you spare me, I promise I will help you one day."

The lion laughed. What could a tiny mouse ever do to help a mighty lion? But he felt kind, so he lifted his paw and let the mouse go free.

Some days later, the lion was walking through the forest when — SNAP! — he fell into a hunter's net. He roared and struggled, but he could not get free.

The little mouse heard the roaring. He ran to help. With his sharp little teeth, he nibbled and nibbled at the ropes until — at last — the net fell apart.

"You laughed when I said I would help you," said the mouse. "But even a small friend can do great things!"

The lion smiled warmly. "You are right, little friend. I will never laugh at a small kindness again."

~160 words · Adapted for age 7

Star Challenge
⭐ STAR QUESTION
Write one question you would like to ask the lion or the mouse.
💡 Imagine you could talk to them! What would you really want to know?
Write your question with a question mark · There's no wrong answer!

Q1 — Retrieval (1 mark)

  • The mouse ran over the lion's nose / ran across the lion's nose. Accept any clear reference to this event. No mark if unrelated.

Q2 — Inference (2 marks)

  • 1 mark: "He felt kind" / the lion had a kind heart / took pity on the mouse.
  • 2 marks: Also references the mouse's promise ("I will help you one day") as a reason, or shows empathy for the mouse.

Q3 — Drawing (1 mark)

  • Any relevant scene drawn. Check it's labelled. 1 mark if any attempt made. Encourage colour and detail as bonus.

Q4 ⭐ — Open response (2 marks)

  • Any genuine, grammatically complete question in correct format (with ?). 1 mark for a question, 2 marks if it shows empathy or curiosity about the story's theme (e.g. "Were you embarrassed that a tiny mouse saved you?" earns full marks).

Total: 6 marks · Points: complete = 8 pts. Bonus: full marks = +2 pts
